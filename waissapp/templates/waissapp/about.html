Our Team

Focus on developing smarter technologies for irrigation and management of crops to help farmers in monitoring their farm and ensuring optimum inputs for loss reduction, high water efficiency, and better yield results.

Part of Project SARAI's advocacy is the practice of smart irrigation – the application of irrigation at the right time at the right amount. The project has developed two systems for soil water monitoring and management: the Water Advisory for Irrigation Scheduling System (WAISS) and the WAISS – Evapotranspiration-based Irrigation Scheduler and Calculator (WAISS-ETCal).

WAISS is a decision support system, which provides site- and crop-specific irrigation advisory and crop water management recommendations.​ The whole system consists of a field unit and computer software. The field unit is installed in the farm for daily moisture content measurement. The server, consisting of a GSM module and software, receives the data from the field unit and analyzes it to generate irrigation advisories. The advisories are then sent to end-users via SMS, daily and during critical periods. End-users may opt to request the current status of their farm by texting “STATUS” to the WAISS server.​

Atmometer measures actual reference evapotranspiration and easily provides data for irrigation scheduling and is comparable to weather stations.​ To have more accurate data, calibration of the atmometer to the local climate conditions must be done, by utilizing empirical equations such as FAO Penman-Monteith. The calibrated reference evapotranspiration reading can be used to estimate the amount of water to irrigate the crop by computing the actual evapotranspiration.​

The team has developed a web application for evapotranspiration-based irrigation scheduling and calculation. I​n this application, the user can create their account based on the characteristics of their farm, such as crop planted and soil texture, and the availability of an atmometer or nearby weather station where evapotranspiration measurements will be computed from. Daily irrigation advisories and schedules are generated through this application, which stakeholders can refer to when scheduling their farm activities.

Our main responsibilities are

  • Develop crop water technologies for our nine (9) priority crops
  • Perform adaptive studies that confirm to the needs of local farmers
  • Optimize our developed technologies for a more accessible smart agriculture
